From this day I could first consider myself as appointed by his Majesty on the Expedition. The Order signed by the King imported that 1795. I should be paid to me by the Exchequer, which I receive accordingly some says afterwards.  
the same day I went to Mr. Banks & not finding him at home I wrote a note to him desiring him to appoint me a day, when I could have the favour to speak to him; for being appointed by his Majesty to go on the Expedition, I wished to be informed by him to me particulars relative to my equipment & having always been favoured with his friendship I hoped, he would add this to the many obligations he had already, laid me under as it was just going away Dr Scharter came in, I told of my going & began likewise his advice, He congratulated are coolly & was civil; but the moment
