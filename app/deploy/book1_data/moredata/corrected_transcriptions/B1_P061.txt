28
July 14° ) The Ships remained in company
the weather continued fair, the wind changed
to W.H.W. & sometimes W. 6. N.N. nor course was SM. 4
S.W.b.S. In the Evening at 9 o'clock we bore
down to the Adventure. In the night the
weather became cloudy & towards morning
heavy with some rain at 8 in the morning
we fired a gun for the Adventure, after
having racked, directing our Course West
S.S. W. SWW. b.W towards noon it grew foggy
& only light airs.
July of 15th We met with a larvae Swell, from
the N.W. & towards night a thick fog co me on
we soon found that we were approaching
the Bay of Biscay, by the great waves. I had
been seasick ever since the first day of one
Voyage, could eat very little, was obliged to
throw up any Virtuals, had no head.each
I found squash much better & greatly
relieved by some mylled Portwine,
We tached on & off till
July 18, when we began to make our course directly
W.b. W. had some squalls & now & then rain
